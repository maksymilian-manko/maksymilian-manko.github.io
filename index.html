PhD Candidate in Mathematics

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Zurich under the supervision of Prof. Anna Beliakova, and a visiting graduate student at Caltech working with Prof. Sergei Gukov. My work is funded by the Simons Collaboration on New Structures in Low-Dimensional Topology and SwissMAP.

My research sits at the intersection of quantum algebra, low-dimensional topology, and machine learning for mathematics. On the pure side I am interested in quantum 4-manifold invariants arising from non-semisimple ribbon categories. On the applied side, I help develop AI (in particular Reinforcement Learning) methods to attack hard open problems in topology and combinatorics.

Research in Mathematics

My work centres on quantum 4-manifold invariants arising from non-semisimple ribbon categories and Hopf algebras, as well as related Topological Quantum Field Theories (TQFTs) constructions.

Building on the success of non-semisimple 3d TQFTs, 4-manifold analogues were recently proposed by Beliakova–Bobtcheva–Piergallini–De Renzi and Costantino–Geer–Haioun–Patureau-Mirand. Both constructions use degenerate, rather than modular, ribbon Hopf algebras and categories, respectively. Beyond this, it is not understood what algebraic data precisely is necessary to produce powerful 4-manifold invariants. Such invariants would be useful in attacking open problems regarding 4-manifolds, such as detection of exotica or the Gompf conjecture, closely related to the Andrews–Curtis conjecture.

My work in Prof. Beliakova's group concentrates on providing novel examples of ribbon Hopf algebras powering the two constructions, as well as understanding the topological information both detect. This leads to the study of new examples of braided tensor categories, Hopf algebra objects therein as well as skein theory bridging the algebraic and topological sides of the question. There are also rich connections with representation theory, higher algebra, and more.

Research in Artificial Intelligence

Building on the ongoing interest in AI, in recent years the area of AI for pure mathematics has seen a steady expansion. The main goal is to develop robust AI models tailor-made for hard problems in mathematics and physics, capable of delivering rigorous results, be it as counterexamples or proofs. This often brings mutual benefit to pure AI research in the form of novel approaches deeply rooted in mathematics.

Within this framework, my work in Prof. Gukov's group concentrates on applying Reinforcement Learning, often hybridised with deterministic methods, to hard problems in topology and combinatorics, such as the Andrews–Curtis conjecture. We aim to maximise the mutual benefit by producing rigorous mathematical results while introducing new architectures in RL. A lot of emphasis is also put on understanding the mathematical data underlying these problems, in order to inform the designs of the models.
